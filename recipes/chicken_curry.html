Recipe Chicken Curry

Pic Credits: Charles Haynes

Description

Chicken curry is a globally popular dish originating from the Indian subcontinent, featuring tender chicken pieces simmered in a rich, aromatic gravy. It combines a tomato and onion base with spices like turmeric, cumin, coriander, ginger, and garlic. Often served with rice or naan

Ingredients

  • Chicken: 500g (about 1 lb) boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts, cut into bite-sized chunks.
  • Cooking Oil 2 tbsp vegetable or canola oil
  • Aromatics: 1 large onion(finely chopped), 2 cloves garlic(minced), and 2 tsp minced fresh ginger
  • Spices: 2 tbsp mild or medium curry powder, 1 tbsp ground coriander, 1/2 tbsp ground cumin, 1 tbsp paprika, 1/2 tbsp ground cinnamon and salt/pepper to taste.
  • Liquids/Base: 2 tbsp Tomato Puree, 1 tin chopped Tomatoes, 240 ml chicken stock, 490 ml full fat coconut milk.

Steps to Cook

  1. Sear the Chicken: Heat the oil in a large skillet or pan over medium-high heat. Add the chicken pieces and cook for 5 minutes until sealed and lightly browned on all sides. Remove and set aside (optional, but helps keep chicken tender).
  2. Saute Aromatics : Reduce heat to medium. Add the chopped onions to the same pan and cook for 5-7 minutes until softened and golden. Add the garlic, ginger, curry powder, coriander, cumin, paprika, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Cook for another 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly to toast the spices.
  3. Build the Sauce: Add the tomato puree, canned tomatoes, chicken stock, and coconut milk. Stir well to combine, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan.
  4. Simmer: Return the chicken (and any juices) to the pan. Bring to a gentle boil, then turn down the heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes until the chicken is fully cooked and the sauce has thickened.
  5. Finish and Serve: If you prefer a thicker sauce, stir in the cornflour slurry (optional). Stir in the baby spinach (if using) until wilted. Garnish with fresh coriander.
  6. Serve: Serve hot over steamed basmati rice or with naan bread.
